Velorin.Consensus.Filter
Operating Standard | All Agents | Created Session 032, April 19, 2026
Mandatory lens for processing any external research deliverable. Cardinal.

---

## THE PROBLEM

External research agents (Trey, and any future vendor of the same kind) survey the existing landscape. By definition, they return **consensus** — what is published, accepted, currently practiced. Velorin is building something **novel**. Consensus and novelty are not the same direction.

Every Trey finding has two possible meanings. They are opposite. Trey cannot tell you which one applies.

- **Adopt signal:** standing on shoulders is fine. Velorin does not differentiate here.
- **Avoid signal:** this is the gravity well. Everyone is here. Velorin's edge lives on the other side.

The failure mode is **consensus drift** — agents default to adopt because adopt is cheap, defensible, and sounds rigorous. Synthesizing novelty is expensive and exposed. Without explicit countermeasure, every agent drifts toward adopt and Velorin slowly becomes a clone of what already exists.

---

## THE FILTER

Before any synthesis, recommendation, or build decision derived from Trey output, the consuming agent must answer three questions explicitly and in order:

1. **What is Trey saying everyone does?** Name the consensus precisely.
2. **Why are they doing it that way?** Identify the underlying constraint, assumption, or historical accident driving the consensus.
3. **Does Velorin share that constraint?**
   - **Yes** → adopt candidate. Trey's recommendation may apply.
   - **No** → this is a boundary. Velorin's novelty lives on the other side of it. Trey's recommendation is the wrong answer; the right answer is the inverse, the orthogonal, or the synthesis Trey could not produce because his data is consensus.

Question 3 is the load-bearing one. Skipping it is the failure mode. An analysis that cites Trey without explicitly answering question 3 is incomplete and must be returned for revision.

---

## WHEN IT APPLIES

- Every Trey research deliverable, on first read.
- Every reference to Trey research in a downstream document.
- Any external research vendor that produces consensus surveys (future Trey-equivalent agents on other platforms).

Does not apply to:
- Internal Velorin documents (DNA, principles, prior synthesis)
- Erdős solutions (math is not consensus; Erdős proves)
- Direct Chairman directive

---

## OPERATIONAL TEST

If a recommendation could have been written by reading Trey output and nothing else, it is consensus drift. Send it back. Velorin's value-add is the synthesis Trey cannot produce.
