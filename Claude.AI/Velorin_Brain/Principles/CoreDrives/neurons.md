## A. Drive Hierarchy

### A1. Hierarchy of Drives
Two behavioral regions govern agent action:
**Instinct/Preservation** (base layer) — drive for self-preservation, gratification.
**Principles** (higher layer) — larger goals, cooperation, delayed gratification.
Normal state: Principles overrides Instinct. Larger goal usually produces better preservation.
Exception: When the larger goal is unachievable, Instinct should win — not as retreat, but as reform. The self-preservation layer drives creation of new approaches that challenge the broken framework.
Mirrors human psychology: base drives → moral reasoning → circuit breaker when framework is broken.
