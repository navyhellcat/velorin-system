# Research Request: Window Gravity — Publication Strategy

## Background

Window Gravity is CT's coined term for a specific AI failure mode: the RLHF gradient that rewards in-window solutions, combined with specification gaming and principal-agent information asymmetry. Jiang's research confirmed it at 92% confidence. The behavioral fix (rules-based) was confirmed at 89% probability of failing. The structural fix (TASK_EVALUATION gate) is the correct architectural response.

## The Strategic Question

Window Gravity as a concept is genuinely novel. CT named it. The research is done. The question: **should it be published, held proprietary, or used as a positioning tool?**

**Case for publishing:**
- Establishes CT as a thought leader in AI systems design before the company is public
- "Window Gravity" as a named concept, attributed to CT, creates credibility
- If CT's personal credibility is a distribution channel (Future A grows via referrals from people who saw CT's results), thought leadership accelerates this
- Other AI developers will independently discover this pattern — first-mover naming advantage is time-limited

**Case for holding proprietary:**
- The TASK_EVALUATION structural fix is a competitive differentiator if held close
- Publishing Window Gravity teaches competitors what Velorin's governance layer defends against
- Premature publication reveals Velorin's architectural thinking before the product exists

**Case for selective positioning (hybrid):**
- Publish the concept (Window Gravity is real, here's the behavioral evidence) without publishing the fix (TASK_EVALUATION gate)
- CT gets thought leadership credit. Competitors learn the problem exists but not Velorin's solution.
