# Research Request: Emergent Taxonomy from the Claude Skills Ecosystem

## Context

The Claude Code skills ecosystem (Anthropic official + large community) contains
13,000+ skills organized independently by many different people and communities.
These communities did not coordinate. They each built organizational taxonomies
that felt natural to them.

I want to understand what organizational structure NATURALLY EMERGES from this
independent, uncoordinated curation — not by reading the category labels people
imposed, but by understanding what actually clusters when you look at the skills
functionally.

The goal: determine whether the community-discovered skill taxonomy could inform
the initial Region/Area structure for a personal AI knowledge graph — and if so,
what that taxonomy actually is.

## Key Repositories to Analyze

- `github.com/anthropics/skills` — Anthropic official (canonical reference)
- `github.com/alirezarezvani/claude-skills` — 248 production skills, 9 primary domains
- `github.com/VoltAgent/awesome-agent-skills` — 1000+ cross-platform
- `github.com/hesreallyhim/awesome-claude-code` — large curated list
- `github.com/obra/superpowers` — 20+ battle-tested workflow skills
- Others you find through Discovery Mode research

## Specific Research Questions

**Q1:** For the top 5-7 repositories: what organizational structure did each community
choose? What are the top-level categories? What are the second-level categories?
Are they organizing by domain (Engineering, Marketing), by cognitive operation
(analyze, generate, transform, extract), by workflow phase (plan, execute, review),
or by something else?

**Q2:** When you look at the actual skill DESCRIPTIONS rather than the imposed category
labels — what concepts appear most frequently across many different categories?
Which skills are cross-domain hubs (appearing relevant to many domains)?
Which skills are highly domain-specific (relevant to only one area)?

**Q3:** Do independent communities (who didn't coordinate) converge on the same
organizational structure? Specifically: does alirezarezvani's taxonomy match
VoltAgent's? Does Anthropic's official taxonomy match the community's emergent one?
What does the convergence or divergence tell us about how humans naturally
partition task knowledge?

**Q4:** Is there a mapping between the emergent skill taxonomy and any framework
from cognitive science for how humans organize procedural/semantic memory?
I am NOT asking you to force a mapping — I am asking whether one exists naturally
when you look at both simultaneously.

**Q5 — DISCOVERY MODE:** What organizational patterns, principles, or insights
exist in this ecosystem that I haven't named in this prompt? What would a skilled
taxonomist, librarian, or knowledge organization researcher observe about how this
community has structured their collective task knowledge? What surprised you?

## What I Actually Need

One of three conclusions (confidence-rated):

(A) The emergent skill taxonomy, when examined carefully, reveals a principled
    organizational structure that could seed the Velorin Brain's Region/Area design.
    If this is the conclusion: what IS that structure, specifically?

(B) The taxonomies are too domain-specific (software engineering, marketing, etc.)
    to generalize to a personal operating system covering all life domains.
    The community skill taxonomy is useful as a reference but not as a template.

(C) Something unexpected that changes how I'm thinking about the question entirely.

Either A, B, or C is useful. Confidence-rate everything. Do not force A if the
evidence supports B or C.
