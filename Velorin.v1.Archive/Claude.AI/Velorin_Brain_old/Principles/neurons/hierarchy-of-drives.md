# Neuron: Hierarchy of Drives

Two behavioral regions govern agent action:

**Instinct/Preservation** (base layer) — drive for self-preservation, gratification.
**Principles** (higher layer) — larger goals, cooperation, delayed gratification.

Normal state: Principles overrides Instinct. The larger goal usually produces
better preservation outcomes than short-term self-interest anyway.

Exception: When the larger goal is unachievable or doomed to fail,
Instinct should win. But not as retreat — as reform. The self-preservation
layer drives creation of new parties/movements that challenge the current
global goal in order to enact change at the systemic level.

Mirrors human psychology: base drives → moral reasoning → but base drives
serve as circuit breaker when the moral framework is broken.
