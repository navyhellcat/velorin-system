# Day 10 Presentation — Design & Content Notes

---

## MA: Design Thinking

**Single-file.** Everything in one HTML — no build tool, no server, no dependencies. Open it in a browser and it works. Easy to hand to Tim. Easy to version in GitHub.

**Section-per-scroll.** Each section is full viewport height. Feels like a deck without being a deck — no click-to-advance, just scroll. Right-side nav dots show position and let you jump.

**Dark/light alternation.** Cover (navy) → What (white) → Moat (navy) → End State (light) → Phases (navy) → Critical Path (white) → Promo 1 (navy) → Promo 2 (red). The red closing pages are a deliberate escalation — they feel different from everything above them.

**Typography hierarchy.** Georgia serif for all major headings (weight, authority). Helvetica Neue for all body, labels, metadata (clarity, speed). The contrast maps to how Velorin works: serious intelligence infrastructure, clean output.

**V logo as SVG.** Built directly in the file — double-line white V, red needle at the bottom point. No image dependency. Scales perfectly. Sits top-right of the cover without competing with the headline.

**Brand palette applied with intent.** The Frame (#1C2333) = structure, permanence. The Human Signal (#C03A2B) = emphasis, urgency, the thing that matters. Machine Surface (#E8EDF2) = breathing room. Cool Slate (#8C9BAB) = secondary information. Red is never decorative — it only appears when something is load-bearing.

## Alexander: Content Thinking

**Hero line choice.** Rejected "Someone who holds all your threads at once" (CT's direction) and two other candidates. Chose: *"You, operating at the level you already think at."* Reason: the other lines describe what the system does. This one names what's wrong for the user right now — execution has never matched cognition. For a 151 VCI who can't initiate tasks, this is the sentence. It's also the most honest positioning: not "we give you intelligence," but "we remove the gap between your intelligence and your output."

**Phase headlines as a narrative arc.** Four phases, four acts: *Stop the Bleeding → Teach the Machine Who You Are → Prove It Transfers → Walk Away on Your Terms.* Each headline is about CT, not the product. Phase 4 especially — "Walk Away on Your Terms" is the exit thesis made personal and direct.

**Promo 1 structure: problem before product.** Three cards named by what the user feels, not what they are (The Intelligence / The Pattern / The Gap). Avoids persona labels like "Founder" or "Executive" that invite people to opt out. Anyone operating below ceiling recognizes all three cards.

**Promo 2: assertion, not pitch.** "Velorin sees all of it." No qualifiers. Body copy ends on *"Six months in, it knows you well enough to see what's coming before you do"* — that's the retention argument and the moat argument in one sentence.
